❖ Common Name: Cherry Blossom Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Prunus serrulata

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Rosaceae

Order: Rosales

Soil Type

Prefers well-drained, fertile, sandy, or loamy soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH. Good drainage is essential to prevent root rot.

Life Span

Cherry blossoms generally have a lifespan of 30 to 50 years. However, some varieties may live longer in favorable conditions.

Pests and Diseases

  • Pests: Aphids, caterpillars, and Japanese beetles cause leaf damage and weaken the tree.
  • Diseases: Prone to fungal diseases like leaf spot, powdery mildew, and root rot in poorly drained soils.

Medical Uses

  • Skin Care: Cherry blossom extracts are used in cosmetics for their antioxidant and anti-aging properties.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Flower extracts have mild anti-inflammatory effects in traditional medicine.
  • Blood Circulation: Believed to improve blood circulation and support heart health.
  • Stress Relief: Cherry blossom petals are sometimes used in teas for their calming effects.
